Spatial Predictive Coding Network (SPCN)A biologically-inspired, 3D spatially-constrained neural network operating on predictive coding principles with fully local updates and no global backpropagation.Architecture & ConceptThe SPCN maps a multi-layer neural network onto a 3D physical coordinate lattice ($W \times H \times D$). Synaptic connectivity is governed by a strict Euclidean distance penalty, mimicking the metabolic and structural constraints of cortical wiring profiles found in biological brains:$$\text{Wiring Cost} \propto \Delta x^2 + \Delta y^2 + \gamma \Delta z^2$$Learning is purely local, optimizing states and synaptic weights using localized prediction error nodes ($e = \text{state} - \text{prediction}$) rather than backward-propagated global gradients.Phase 2 Empirical Results & Post-MortemPhase 2 evaluated the SPCN on the real MNIST dataset (28×28 pixels, 10 classes) mapped into a $28 \times 28 \times 4$ voxel grid ($109,760$ parameters).While vectorization yielded high execution throughput (~3.65 iterations/sec, ~2.5s wall time per epoch), scaling up the input data exposed a series of fundamental algorithmic challenges. Below is the documentation of the architectural diagnostic path and the final structural verdict.1. Fixed-Point Collapse (The "Bayesian Blur")Symptom: Initial test runs resulted in random chance accuracy (~11-13%). State-space tracing showed that Layer 1 activations collapsed to an identical fixed point across all samples, reducing spatial variance to near zero ($\sigma \approx 0.0024$).Root Cause: In high-density 3D voxel grids with traditional small initial weights ($~N(0, 0.01)$), the local prediction step acts as a massive spatial low-pass filter. Summing over $\sim 75$ local neighbors averages out high-frequency spatial variation (Law of Large Numbers), causing the sigmoid activation function to contract toward a uniform scalar.Resolution: Implemented localized Kaiming/Xavier initialization scaled to neighborhood fan-in, successfully stabilizing state variance ($\sigma \approx 0.0225$) and preserving image-dependent signals through the settle loop.2. The Hard-Clamp Short-CircuitSymptom: After applying proper weight scaling, training accuracy instantly hit 100%, but test accuracy remained pinned at a flat 12%.Root Cause: Inspecting the training loop revealed that clamp_top_layer maintained a hard force (blend=1.0) on the classification layer up to the final settle iteration. The post-hoc linear readout head (digit_weights) trivially learned a fixed lookup mapping (label prototype $\rightarrow$ label), completely bypassing the hidden representations derived from the image input.Resolution: Implemented an annealed/fading clamp protocol (stepping down blend from 1.0 to 0.0 over 8 settle iterations). This forced the top layer to release its anchor and rely entirely on the bottom-up signals routed from the input canvas during the final readout phase.The Definitive Verdict: Mode CollapseFollowing the stabilization of internal grid dynamics and the decoupling of the training short-circuit, the system achieved the following metrics:Training Loss: Monotonically decreased ($0.1804 \rightarrow 0.099$).Classification Accuracy: Stuck at chance level (~10-12%) on both Train and Test sets.Empirical Proof via Argmax DistributionA deep trace of the linear readout layer's classification predictions across 200 distinct evaluation samples yielded a clean Mode Collapse:PlaintextPredicted class counts: Counter({5: 200})

Theoretical FoundationThis outcome exposes a structural boundary well-documented in modern predictive coding literature (Whittington & Bogacz, 2017; Millidge et al., 2020).Pure, unsupervised predictive coding architectures optimize exclusively for local reconstruction error. When two distinct classes (such as a handwritten "3" and "8") share up to 85% of their spatial topology, a purely generative local update rule organizes weights to represent the shared pixel layout. It contains no objective function or pressure mechanism to suppress shared variance and amplify the tiny, 15% semantic discrepancies required for discrete classification.Future Trajectory: Phase 3 (Contrastive Learning)To shatter this representational ceiling while maintaining strict adherence to the "fully local learning" constraint, the SPCN architecture must transition from a purely generative paradigm to a Contrastive/Bidirectional Predictive Coding framework.The next development phase will implement a Contrastive Hebbian Learning (CHL) protocol:Positive Phase: The network settles with both the sensory input and the target class label gently clamped, capturing the joint distribution.Negative Phase: The network free-runs without target clamping, generating its own internal prediction.Local Weight Update: Synaptic adjustments are driven by the local divergence between the two steady states:$$\Delta W \propto e_{\text{positive}} - e_{\text{negative}}$$This introduces the explicit push-pull competitive dynamics needed to force different semantic classes into isolated, linearly-separable attractors within the 3D voxel lattice.ReferencesRao, R.
